## Remember DUWOP

This era of digital file-sharing poses a challenge for our special community. Many of us give ourselves permission while at Eliot to try new things, stretch in new directions, risk unfamiliar behaviors. This is one of the joys of what we create together.

At the same time, not all of us are comfortable sharing these brave acts beyond Eliot. And posting of photos and videos can, easily and inadvertently, result in embarrassment or other negative consequences. We get to tell our own stories… we let our friends tell their own stories.

In an attempt to balance this need for privacy with the desire for continued fun and connection, Eliot has a policy about image sharing. This is: DUWOP – Don’t Upload With-Out Permission. Before posting photos or videos of any camper to the Internet (or transmitting via cell phone), you must first obtain permission from the subject; for children, you must obtain permission from the parents.

## Online Registration and Best Way to Pay

Want to come to Eliot again next year? Of course you do! To register for next summer’s camps, please go online to www.eliotinstitute.org and click on the Big Blue Button to register. The registration process is complete with your electronic signature and payment of your deposit ($50/camper) via credit card. Many campers wonder if it would be better to pay their deposit via check or cash. While we do pay a 2.2% surcharge for credit card transactions, deposit amounts are small, so the convenience to me of having the registration and deposit directly linked is worth it. Paying by check or cash is ok, but tracking the online registration form on my computer and a physical envelope with checks and cash can be a logistical challenge. However, once your invoice arrives for the balance due for camp, checks are preferred, because that 2.2% PayPal surcharge on larger balances really adds up and becomes a more significant part of our budget.
