<Article title="Departure Day Dos and Don'ts">

_Be sure to return your room keys to the basket on the counter of the Eliot Office._ Don’t just leave them in your room. When left alone in a vacant room, keys become desperately lonely and run helter-skelter to places unknown looking for companionship. They can’t find their way by themselves to the basket of their companions at the Eliot Office, **so please, please, PLEASE, turn your room keys in at the Eliot Office.**

**DO** check in with your house leader to find out what area of the grounds you are responsible to help clean up, and **DO** check the Lost and Found for any of your items. **And, please, please, pretty please - DO return your room keys to the Eliot Office before you go home.**

</Article>

<Article title="Eagle Eye Elioteers!">
The Eliot Newsletter arrives in your e-mailbox twice a year—in October and April. Inside you’ll find reports on camps, Board news, financial reports, and a peek behind the scenes at all the work, dedication, and love that help keep the Eliot traditions alive for generations to come.

One of the most treasured parts of the newsletter is our Eagle Eye section. Just like gathering in the Inn to catch up with old friends, it’s a place to celebrate life’s milestones and share in one another’s journeys. We’d love to hear about births, graduations, weddings, new adventures, relocations, retirements, special accomplishments, the passing of loved ones, or any news you’d like your Eliot family to know. Every story helps keep the bonds of camp strong and reminds us that, while we may not all be together on the shores of Hood Canal we’re always connected in spirit.

Please send your updates at any time, subject line Eliot News, to Jean DeVenney, at mizznizz@gmail.com. Thank you for helping us keep the Eliot connections going year ‘round.

</Article>

<Article title="Fall Prevention Follow-up" attribution="Chris Conyers">

Thank you to all the participants in the fall prevention workshop. In a nutshell:

- 10 min walk after 1 to 3 meals/day
- Sit no more than 30 mins.
- Strength, Power (jumping, fast feet, explosive lifts, and plyometrics), hard intervals (Norwegian 4x4s), easy aerobic (a nice walk with friends)
- Resistance training pearls.

- Exercise snacks: walk and talk, microwave pushups, one legged stand before coffee, squats while waiting, jumps off a step, heel raises. One or more done 3 mins/day, or
- Foundational resistance exercises: squats, deadlifts, farmer’s carry, overhead presses, planks, get ups done once per week. And all of the variations.

Call me for details or better, get a trainer like Kristi!

</Article>
