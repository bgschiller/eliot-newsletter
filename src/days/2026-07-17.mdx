<Article title="Come Back Next Year">

Join us again for July Eliot 2026! The theme speaker will be Lynn Ungar, with a program called “**With Love at the Center: The Art and Science of Living Our Values**” Please visit our website at www.eliotinsititute.org to find out more about the program, then click on the Big Blue Button to register. **(Note: room assignments happen in order of registration, so registering early will greatly improve your odds of getting the housing you want!)**
</Article>

<Article title="Evaluations">
Have ideas on what could’ve gone better, or want to praise those things that went well? Please share your thoughts with us! Camp evaluations for adults will coming to your email! If you have someone in the children's or MAG’s program, evaluation forms will be slipped under your door before the end of camp. Please encourage them to fill them out.
</Article>

<Article title="Sunday Brunch">
Seabeck will be providing us with a brunch and sandwich bar, open to campers from 8:15 - 10:15. Schedule your morning however you'd like - eat early and then pack up or pack up first before enjoying your morning meal. If you'd like, you're also welcome to make a sandwich to sustain you on your way
home.

After brunch, please join us for our closing gathering in the Meeting House. When this gathering ends and you've bid your fond farewells to friends new and old, it'll be time to head back home over the rumbly bridge. Safe travels!
</Article>

<Article title="Name Tag Recycling">

We can't reuse your name tag and hand them to different campers (those neck cords are filled with your neck sweat, people!), BUT you can reuse your own next year by bringing them to camp, and returning the unused sleeve to us when you check-in.
</Article>

<Article title="Departure Day Dos and Don'ts">

_Be sure to return your room keys to the basket on the counter of the Eliot Office._ Don’t just leave them in your room. When left alone in a vacant room, keys become desperately lonely and run helter-skelter to places unknown looking for companionship. They can’t find their way by themselves to the basket of their companions at the Eliot Office, **so please, please, PLEASE, turn your room keys in at the Eliot Office.**

**DO** check in with your house leader to find out what area of the grounds you are responsible to help clean up, and **DO** check the Lost and Found for any of your items. **And, please, please, pretty please - DO return your room keys to the Eliot Office before you go home.**
</Article>
