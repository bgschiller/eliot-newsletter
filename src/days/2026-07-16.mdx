<Article title="The Oldest Song in the World" attribution="Chris Struble">
Here are some interesting facts about the medley of ancient music I played in the concert yesterday.

The Seikilos Epitaph is the oldest surviving complete song in the world. It was found in T&uuml;rkiye in 1883 by Irish engineer Edward Purse, on a stone pillar about 3 feet high, with an inscription in ancient Greek. Scholars date the inscription to the 1st or 2nd century C.E., and interpret it as an epitaph for Euterpe, the deceased wife of Seikilos, the man who wrote it. It would have been played on a lyre, an ancient musical instrument. Mr. Purse’s wife used the pillar as a flower pot stand in their home for a time. The pillar is now safely in a museum in Denmark.

</Article>
