<Article title="Angel from Montgomery" attribution="Brian Schiller">

I have some questions about the John Prine song, Angel from Montgomery. You know when it says, "There's flies in the kitchen. I can here 'em there buzzing." Are we sure about the spelling of "there"? It could be

- I can hear 'em there (in the kitchen) buzzing, or
- I can hear 'em; they're buzzing (I can hear 'em _because_ they are buzzing), or
- I can hear 'em; their buzzing (the buzzing is what we can hear)

Probably not that last one, but the other two seem plausible.

</Article>
